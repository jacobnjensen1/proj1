Codariocalyx motorius

Semaphore Plant, Dancing Plant

About this plant

C. motorius is native to Southeast Asia. It may be possible to grow it indoors in Utah, but it certainly prefers humid areas. This plant is in Fabaceae, the pea family, and has small purple to white pea-like flowers. This plant needs well draining soil in full to partial sun. This plant is used medicinally by some people, but it contains toxic alkaloids, so care should be taken if you want to consume this plant safely.

Why this plant is cool

C. motorius is notable for being capable of rapid plant movement. Its leaves constantly move in circular patterns. It is not known exactly why this is done. Some hypotheses are that it helps the plant optimize leaf position for maximum sunlight, that it reduces the likelihood that butterflies will lay eggs on it by mimicking a butterfly, or that it deter predators. The answer will almost certainly never be known, but it is fascinating that it does this nonetheless.
