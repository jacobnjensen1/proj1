Platycerium superbum

Staghorn Fern

About this plant

P. superbum is native to New South Wales, Australia. It has been introduced to Indonesia, Malaysia, and Hawai'i, where it is considered a "problem species". This plant can be grown indoors in Utah, though it might need extra humidity. In nature, this plant is epiphytic, meaning that it grows on trees, but is not parasitic.

Why this plant is cool

P. superbum is a pretty weird plant. The broad leaves catch humus (leaves and such, not hummus), which decompose in place. P. superbum can then access the nutrients present in the humus. This "nest" can be around 3 feet wide, with 6 foot long fronds dangling below it.
