Welwitschia mirabilis

Tree Tumbo, Welwitschia

About this plant

W. mirabilis is native to the Namib desert in southern Africa. It grows slowly, but can live extremely long. Some estimates put the oldest welwitschias over 2000 years old, although it is difficult to date these plants. They are dioeceous, meaning that each plant only produces either male or female cones.

Why this plant is cool

W. mirabilis is a remarkable plant. It only produces two leaves, yet can grow to be 5 feet tall and 26 feet wide. These are the only living members of the Welwitschiales order, although there were once 3 other species in the order that were native to Brazil. These exctinct species are only known through the fossil record. Due to this, W. mirabilis is commonly referred to as a "living fossil".
